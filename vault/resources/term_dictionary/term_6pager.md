# 6-Pager (Six-Page Narrative)

## Definition

A **6-Pager** (Six-Page Narrative) is Amazon's standard document format for strategic proposals, business cases, operational reviews, and decision-making. The document consists of up to **six pages of narrative prose** (no bullets) plus **unlimited appendices**. At the start of meetings, attendees read the document **silently for 20-30 minutes** before discussion begins, ensuring everyone has the same context and can engage in informed debate.

The 6-pager embodies Amazon's belief that "writing is thinking" - forcing authors to clarify their ideas, structure their arguments logically, and provide complete context. Unlike PowerPoint presentations that allow presenters to gloss over details, written narratives require thoroughness and precision.

## Core Philosophy

### Why Writing Over Presentations

> "The traditional kind of corporate meeting starts with a presentation. Somebody gets up in front of the room and presents with a powerpoint presentation, some type of slide show. In our view you get very little information, you get bullet points. This is easy for the presenter, but difficult for the audience. And so instead, all of our meetings are structured around a 6 page narrative memo.... If you have a traditional ppt presentation, executives interrupt. If you read the whole 6 page memo, on page 2 you have a question but on page 4 that question is answered." - **Jeff Bezos**

**Key Principles**:

1. **Writing Forces Clarity**: You cannot hide unclear thinking in prose
2. **Complete Context**: Everyone reads the same information at their own pace
3. **Better Questions**: Questions after reading entire document are better informed
4. **Durable**: Documents outlive their author and the moment in time
5. **Substance Over Style**: Emphasis on content, not presentation skills
6. **Democratic**: Junior and senior voices equally weighted based on quality of writing

### The Six-Page Limit

**Why Six Pages Maximum**:
- **Forces Conciseness**: Writers must prioritize and be crisp
- **Moves Detail to Appendices**: Supporting analysis separate from main narrative
- **Ensures Readability**: Can be read in 20-30 minutes
- **Maintains Focus**: Long enough for depth, short enough to stay focused

**Critical Rules**:
- ✅ **Six pages of narrative maximum** (strictly enforced)
- ✅ **Unlimited appendices** for supporting data/analysis
- ✅ **Narrative prose** - full sentences, no bullets
- ✅ **Self-contained** - appendices are reference only, narrative stands alone
- ❌ **Cannot exceed six pages** even by single sentence
- ❌ **No moving critical content to appendices** to game the limit

## Key Highlights

**Document Structure and Writing**: A 6-pager follows a standard structure -- Purpose/Executive Summary (recommendation upfront), Background/Context, Problem/Situation, Recommendation/Solution, Implementation Plan, and Appendices -- with alternative layouts for technical designs, operational reviews, and strategic proposals. Narrative prose with full sentences is mandatory; bullet points, jargon, weasel words, and passive voice are prohibited. High-quality documents are data-driven, self-contained, logically flowing, and state conclusions immediately.

**Meeting Ritual and Application Scope**: The silent reading ritual (20-30 minutes of complete silence at meeting start, followed by discussion where the document speaks for itself) ensures equal footing, thoughtful questions, and focused debate. 6-pagers are used across strategic planning (OP1/OP2, QBR, MBR), technical decision-making (architecture proposals, tool evaluations), operational excellence (process changes, COEs), and product strategy -- but not for PR/FAQs, one-pagers, or status updates.

**Cultural Impact and Format Differentiation**: The 6-pager differentiates Amazon from PowerPoint-driven companies by forcing critical thinking, leveling the playing field for junior writers, creating durable institutional knowledge, and scaling decision-making asynchronously. Compared to one-pagers (quick alignment) and PR/FAQs (customer validation), the 6-pager serves deep decision-making. Writing typically takes 3-4 weeks with 5-10+ drafts, supported by tools like Doc Bar Raiser Reviewer, WeaselWords Macro, and training courses (Narratives 101/201).
