# Fetzenwissen

## Definition

**Fetzenwissen** (German: "scrap knowledge" or "fragmentary knowledge") is a term coined by Austrian satirist Karl Kraus in 1907 to describe knowledge composed of disconnected fragments — superficially gathered facts that lack coherent integration. In the Zettelkasten context, Bob Doto identifies Fetzenwissen as one of eight common note-taking mistakes: creating notes that capture isolated snippets or highlights without developing them into complete, contextualised thoughts. A note suffering from Fetzenwissen is technically "atomic" in size but lacks the conceptual depth that atomicity is meant to serve.

## Historical Context

Karl Kraus introduced the term in his Viennese satirical magazine *Die Fackel* (1907) to criticize the superficial erudition produced by newspaper culture — people who knew many disconnected facts but understood little. Bob Doto revived the concept in *A System for Writing* (2024), applying it to Zettelkasten practice to diagnose notes that are mere highlights or quotes without the writer's own thinking.

## Key Properties

- Notes are short but shallow — size mimics atomicity without achieving it
- Content is extracted rather than processed — highlights, quotes, or paraphrases without original thought
- Missing the writer's own interpretation, context, or connections
- Creates an illusion of productivity (many notes) without genuine knowledge development
- Contrasts with properly atomic notes where each captures one *developed* knowledge building block
- Related to Sascha's Concept building block — Fetzenwissen occurs when notes lack proper conceptual grounding

## Related Terms

- **[Atomicity](term_atomicity.md)**: The principle Fetzenwissen superficially mimics — one idea per note, but properly developed
- **[Knowledge Building Blocks](term_knowledge_building_blocks.md)**: The framework for what should anchor each note; Fetzenwissen lacks a clear building block
- **[Permanent Notes](term_permanent_notes.md)**: The opposite of Fetzenwissen — fully developed, self-contained notes
- **[Fleeting Notes](term_fleeting_notes.md)**: Raw captures that should be developed; Fetzenwissen is what happens when they aren't
- **[Literature Notes](term_literature_notes.md)**: Intermediate processing step that prevents Fetzenwissen when done properly
- **[Verknüpfungszwang](term_verknupfungszwang.md)**: Another Doto anti-pattern — compulsive linking; often co-occurs with Fetzenwissen
- **[Progressive Summarization](term_progressive_summarization.md)**: Forte's highlighting method that can produce Fetzenwissen if not paired with original thinking
