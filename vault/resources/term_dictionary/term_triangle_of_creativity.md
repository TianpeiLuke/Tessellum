# Triangle of Creativity

## Definition

The **Triangle of Creativity** is a framework introduced by Bob Doto in *A System for Writing* (2024) describing the three interdependent activities that drive knowledge development in a Zettelkasten practice: **reading**, **thinking**, and **writing**. The triangle emphasises that these activities must be continuously integrated — not performed in isolated phases. A practitioner who reads without writing, writes without thinking, or thinks without reading breaks the creative cycle. The Zettelkasten serves as the medium where all three activities converge and reinforce each other.

## Key Properties

- **Three vertices**: Reading (input), Thinking (processing), Writing (output) — each feeds the others
- **Continuous integration**: All three happen concurrently, not sequentially — reading prompts writing, writing reveals gaps in thinking, thinking guides reading
- **Anti-phase model**: Rejects the common "first research, then write" approach in favour of iterative cycling
- **The Zettelkasten as medium**: The slipbox is where the triangle operates — notes are the tangible residue of the reading-thinking-writing cycle
- **Prevents Fetzenwissen**: When all three vertices are active, notes develop depth rather than remaining fragments
- **Prevents Verknüpfungszwang**: Thinking about connections before making them ensures linking is purposeful
- **Echoes Ahrens**: Parallels Sönke Ahrens' insight in *How to Take Smart Notes* that writing is not the outcome of thinking but the medium of thinking
