# Sleeping Folder

## Definition

A **Sleeping Folder** is a workflow management concept introduced by Bob Doto in *A System for Writing* (2024) for handling inbox overflow in a Zettelkasten practice. When the inbox accumulates more items than can be processed in the current session, items that are not immediately actionable or relevant are moved to the sleeping folder rather than being discarded or left cluttering the inbox. The sleeping folder serves as a low-pressure holding space — items "sleep" until they become relevant again through the practitioner's evolving interests or project needs.

## Key Properties

- **Overflow buffer**: Prevents inbox anxiety by providing a guilt-free place for unprocessed items
- **Not a graveyard**: Items are periodically reviewed and either promoted (back to inbox for processing) or archived
- **Distinct from the inbox**: The inbox is for active processing; the sleeping folder is for deferred items
- **Distinct from archives**: Archives are for completed/retired items; sleeping folders are for items awaiting their moment
- **Reduces decision fatigue**: "Sleep it" is easier than "process it now or delete it forever"
- **Supports hidden relevance**: Items that seem irrelevant today may gain significance as the practitioner's knowledge network grows
- **Periodic review cadence**: Doto recommends reviewing the sleeping folder regularly (weekly or monthly) to check if sleeping items have become relevant
