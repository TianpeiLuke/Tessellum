# Term: Information Cascades

## Definition

**Information cascades** occur when individuals, acting rationally, follow the observed actions of others rather than their own private information — leading to **herding behavior** where the group converges on a choice that may be wrong. A cascade forms when the accumulated public signal (what others have done) overwhelms each individual's private signal (their own information). The key insight from Christian & Griffiths: cascades are **rational at the individual level but potentially catastrophic at the group level** — each person is making the best decision given what they observe, yet the group can converge on an incorrect outcome. Cascades are also **fragile**: they can collapse quickly when new, sufficiently strong contrary information emerges.

## Full Name

**Information Cascades** = Informational Cascade / Herding Behavior

Also known as: **Herding**, **Rational Herding**, **Bandwagon Effect**, **Social Proof Cascade**

## How Information Cascades Form

### The Cascade Mechanism

```
Person 1: Has private signal → Acts on it (no public signal yet)
Person 2: Has private signal + sees Person 1's action → Weighs both
Person 3: Has private signal + sees Persons 1 & 2 → Public signal dominates
Person 4: Has private signal + sees Persons 1, 2, 3 → Ignores own signal, follows crowd
   ...
Person N: Public signal so strong → Private signal irrelevant → CASCADE
```

### When Cascades Form

| Condition | Effect | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Weak private signals** | Cascade forms quickly | Restaurant choice with little personal knowledge |
| **Strong private signals** | Cascade resists forming | Expert decisions based on deep domain knowledge |
| **Sequential observation** | Each person sees prior actions | Movie choice after seeing box office numbers |
| **Simultaneous action** | No cascade possible | Secret ballot voting |

## Key Properties

### Fragility
Cascades are **informationally fragile** — they can collapse as quickly as they form:

| Property | Implication |
|----------|------------|
| **Built on weak foundations** | Cascade may rest on just 1–2 early movers who happened to agree |
| **Easy to break** | A single confident contrarian with strong evidence can shatter the cascade |
| **Cyclical** | Markets, fashions, and opinions can cascade in one direction, then suddenly reverse |
| **Self-correcting (sometimes)** | When a cascade breaks, the new cascade may be correct — or may be another wrong one |

### Social Proof vs. Information Cascades

| Concept | Mechanism | Rationality |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Social proof** (Cialdini) | Follow the crowd as a heuristic | Heuristic — "others know something I don't" |
| **Information cascade** | Rationally discard private info when public signal dominates | Fully rational — Bayesian updating on observed actions |
| **Groupthink** | Conform to group due to social pressure | Irrational — driven by desire to belong, not information |

## Practical Applications

1. **Recognize cascades**: When everyone agrees, ask whether they each independently assessed the evidence or simply followed early movers
2. **Value contrarians**: In cascade-prone situations, dissenting voices carry disproportionate informational value
3. **Delay observation**: To preserve information diversity, have people make independent assessments before sharing (like silent brainstorming before group discussion)
4. **Market bubbles**: Financial bubbles are information cascades — rational individual decisions producing irrational collective outcomes
5. **Product reviews**: Early reviews disproportionately influence later ones; a few early negative reviews can kill a product regardless of true quality
