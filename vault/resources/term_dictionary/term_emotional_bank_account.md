# Emotional Bank Account

## Definition

The **Emotional Bank Account** is a metaphor introduced by **Stephen R. Covey** in *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* (1989) to describe the amount of trust that has been built up in a relationship. Just as a financial bank account stores monetary deposits and withdrawals, the Emotional Bank Account tracks the cumulative balance of positive and negative interactions between two people. A high balance — built through consistent courtesy, kindness, honesty, and keeping commitments — creates a reserve of trust and goodwill that can absorb occasional mistakes or misunderstandings. A low or overdrawn balance means the relationship is fragile, guarded, and easily ruptured by even minor friction.

The concept operationalizes an intuitive truth: **trust is not binary but quantitative**, accumulated through many small interactions rather than established in a single grand gesture. Covey identified six specific types of "deposits" that build the account, and emphasized that withdrawals (broken promises, disrespect, betrayal) deplete trust far faster than deposits build it — mirroring the asymmetry observed in loss aversion and Gottman's research on relationship ratios.

## Key Properties

- **Cumulative and asymmetric**: Trust accrues gradually through many small deposits but can be depleted rapidly by a single large withdrawal; Gottman's research suggests a 5:1 positive-to-negative interaction ratio is needed to maintain healthy relationships
- **Relationship-specific**: Each relationship has its own separate account; a high balance with one person does not transfer to another
- **Context-dependent deposits**: What constitutes a deposit varies by individual — understanding the other person's values and needs is itself the first deposit (Covey's "seek first to understand")
- **Compounding nature**: Consistent deposits over time create a compounding reserve of goodwill, where trust becomes self-reinforcing and relationships become more resilient to stress
- **Withdrawal amplification**: In a low-balance account, even neutral statements are interpreted negatively; in a high-balance account, even clumsy communication is given the benefit of the doubt
- **Applicable across domains**: The metaphor applies equally to personal relationships, leadership, team dynamics, customer relationships, and organizational culture

### Covey's Six Major Deposits

| Deposit | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Understanding the individual** | Genuinely listening to and empathizing with the other person's perspective | Taking time to learn what matters most to a team member |
| **Attending to little things** | Small courtesies, acts of kindness, and thoughtful gestures | Remembering a colleague's important date or preference |
| **Keeping commitments** | Following through on promises, no matter how small | Delivering on a deadline you committed to |
| **Clarifying expectations** | Making expectations explicit and mutual from the start | Aligning on project scope before beginning work |
| **Showing personal integrity** | Being honest, loyal, and consistent — treating absent people with respect | Defending a team member's reputation when they are not present |
| **Apologizing sincerely** | Offering genuine apologies when a withdrawal occurs | Acknowledging a mistake without excuses or deflection |

### Common Withdrawals

| Withdrawal | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Breaking promises | Major trust erosion; signals unreliability |
| Discourtesy or disrespect | Signals that the relationship is not valued |
| Betraying confidences | Catastrophic withdrawal; may overdraw the account entirely |
| Ignoring or dismissing | Makes the other person feel invisible and unimportant |
| Unclear or shifting expectations | Creates confusion, frustration, and a sense of unfairness |

## Related Terms

- [Reciprocity Bias](term_reciprocity_bias.md) — the automatic obligation to return favors and concessions; deposits into the Emotional Bank Account trigger reciprocal trust-building behavior
- [Active Listening](term_active_listening.md) — a core mechanism for Covey's first deposit ("understanding the individual"); active listening simultaneously builds comprehension and relational trust
- [Compound Effect](term_compound_effect.md) — small, consistent deposits compound over time to produce disproportionately strong trust reserves; the Emotional Bank Account is compounding applied to relationships
- [Growth Mindset](term_growth_mindset.md) — the belief that relationships and trust can be developed through effort; a growth mindset sustains the long-term deposit-making behavior required to build high-balance accounts
- [Trusted System](term_trusted_system.md) — trust in systems (GTD, Zettelkasten) compounds through consistent reliability, paralleling how interpersonal trust compounds through consistent deposits
