# Interstitial Journaling

## Definition

**Interstitial Journaling** is a micro-journaling practice coined by Tony Stubblebine (CEO of Medium, formerly coach.me) around 2017. The method involves writing brief journal entries in the gaps between tasks — at the moment of transition from one activity to the next. Rather than maintaining a separate journaling habit, the practice is embedded into existing workflow transitions. Bob Doto incorporates interstitial journaling into his Zettelkasten workflow in *A System for Writing* (2024) as a tool for capturing fleeting thoughts, maintaining creative momentum, and feeding the CLOG.

## Key Properties

- **Transition-triggered**: Entries are written between tasks, not at a dedicated journaling time
- **Brief and informal**: 1-3 sentences capturing what just happened, what's next, or what was noticed
- **Zero additional time cost**: Uses natural pauses that already exist in the workday
- **Captures fleeting thoughts**: Ideas that arise during task transitions — often lost without this practice
- **Feeds other systems**: Entries can become fleeting notes, CLOG entries, or day log content
- **Reduces context-switching cost**: The act of writing during transitions helps close cognitive loops
- **Complements Zettelkasten practice**: Provides raw material for note development without requiring dedicated capture sessions
