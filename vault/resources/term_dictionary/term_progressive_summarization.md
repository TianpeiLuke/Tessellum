# Term: Progressive Summarization

## Definition

**Progressive Summarization** is a multi-layer distillation technique introduced by Tiago Forte in *Building a Second Brain* (2022) for making captured notes discoverable and useful to your future self. Rather than organizing notes by category or tags at capture time, Progressive Summarization adds successive layers of highlighting and compression *over time*, each layer reducing the information by 80–90% so that the most valuable insights float to the surface. The technique is deliberately incremental: Layer 1 happens at capture, but Layers 2–4 are applied only when you revisit the note for a specific purpose — "just-in-time" distillation rather than "just-in-case" organization.

Forte's counterintuitive framing: Progressive Summarization is "a method for *forgetting* as much as possible — when you drop the merely good parts, the great parts can shine more brightly." This inverts the common assumption that good notes preserve everything. Instead, the value comes from *compression* — each layer strips noise and amplifies signal, creating a power-law distribution where the top 1–2% of the content carries 80% of the value.

## The Four Layers

| Layer | Name | Method | Target Size | When Applied |
|-------|------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Soil** | Capture the original excerpt or passage | ~500 words | At capture time |
| 2 | **Oil** | **Bold** the main points within the excerpt | 10–20% of Layer 1 | First revisit |
| 3 | **Gold** | ==Highlight== the most critical passages within the bolded text | 10–20% of Layer 2 | Second revisit or active project use |
| 4 | **Gems** | Write an executive summary in your own words | 1–2 sentences | Only for the most important notes |

**The 10–20% rule**: Each layer should contain no more than 10–20% of the previous layer. A 500-word excerpt becomes ~75 bolded words, ~12 highlighted words, and a 1–2 sentence summary. This exponential compression ensures that even years-old notes can be quickly scanned and understood.

**Layer 4 is rare**: Most notes never reach Layer 4. The executive summary is reserved for the most important, most frequently revisited notes — perhaps 5–10% of your entire collection. Attempting Layer 4 on every note defeats the purpose of incremental processing.

## Comparison with Other Distillation Approaches

| Approach | Compression Model | Processing Timing | Emphasis |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Progressive Summarization** (Forte) | 4 layers within a single note; visual formatting | Just-in-time (when revisited) | Compression and discoverability |
| **Zettelkasten note pipeline** (Luhmann) | 3 note types: fleeting → literature → permanent | At processing time (separate notes) | Connection and integration |
| **Cornell Notes** | 3 regions: notes, cues, summary | During and after lecture | Recall and review |
| **SQ3R** | Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review | During reading | Comprehension and retention |

**Key distinction from Zettelkasten**: The Zettelkasten creates *new notes* at each processing level — the literature note is a separate document from the fleeting note, and the permanent note is separate from both. Progressive Summarization adds *layers within the same note* — the original text is preserved and progressively annotated. Both reduce information; the Zettelkasten emphasizes *inter-note connection*, while Progressive Summarization emphasizes *intra-note compression*.

## Design Principles

### 1. Design for Your Future Self

Every note should be written (and formatted) for a person who has forgotten the context — your future self. Bold and highlight text not for emphasis during reading, but for *scannability* during retrieval. The question is not "What's important right now?" but "What will I need to understand in six months?"

### 2. Just-in-Time Processing

Progressive Summarization rejects the "process everything at capture" philosophy. Most captured notes will never be revisited. Investing deep processing effort into every note is wasted energy. Instead, add layers only when you actually need the note — when it surfaces in the context of a project. This is **lazy evaluation** applied to knowledge management.

### 3. Opportunistic Compression

Each encounter with a note is an opportunity to add a layer. Reading a note for Project A? Bold the relevant passages. Returning to it for Project B? Highlight the passages relevant to this new context. The note accumulates layers of meaning over time, each layer reflecting a different use case.

## Applications

### Knowledge Management

In the vault, Progressive Summarization principles appear in the digest note format: the Overview section is a Layer 4 summary, the Key Takeaways section is a Layer 3 compression, and the full body is Layer 1–2. Entry point notes function as Layer 4 summaries across multiple notes — one-line descriptions that let you scan dozens of resources in seconds.

### Research and Paper Digestion

The paper digestion pipeline mirrors Progressive Summarization: the literature note's Summary section is a Layer 4 executive summary, each section note (intro, algo, results) is Layer 2–3 compression, and the original PDF is Layer 1. The `<!-- VERIFY -->` markers flag sections needing deeper processing — analogous to notes that haven't yet reached Layer 3.
