# CLOG (Creative Log / Catalog Log)

## Definition

A **CLOG** (Creative Log or Catalog Log) is a workflow management tool introduced by Bob Doto in *A System for Writing* (2024) for tracking active Zettelkasten projects. Unlike a daily log (chronological journal) or a traditional to-do list, a CLOG is organized by project — each entry captures what was worked on, what was discovered, and what needs attention next within a specific creative or intellectual project. The CLOG bridges the gap between the Zettelkasten (where ideas live) and the writer's active workflow (where projects progress).

## Key Properties

- **Project-organized, not date-organized**: Entries are grouped by project, not by calendar date
- **Tracks creative process**: Records discoveries, connections found, questions raised — not just tasks completed
- **Complements the Zettelkasten**: The slipbox stores knowledge; the CLOG tracks the writer's relationship to that knowledge over time
- **Captures momentum**: Records what's working, what's stuck, and what to explore next in each project
- **Low friction**: Quick, informal entries — not polished writing
- **Prevents context loss**: When returning to a project after days or weeks, the CLOG provides continuity
- **Distinct from day logs**: Day logs are chronological life journals; CLOGs are project-specific creative journals
